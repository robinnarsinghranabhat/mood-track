# AWEAR Beta — EEG Brain Sensor

## What It Is

Compact ear-worn EEG wearable that monitors neural electrical signals via a companion iOS app.

## What It Measures

- **Stress response** — detects rising stress before conscious awareness
- **Focus levels** — concentration and attention shifts
- **Mental load** — cognitive burden and overwhelm
- **Mental balance/recovery** — when to pause/recharge
- **Daily patterns** — how mind responds to work, rest, everyday life

## Form Factor

- Worn discreetly behind the ear
- Adhesive patches (~7 days each, 5 included)
- USB-C rechargeable
- Designed for continuous all-day wear

## Technical Details

- EEG electrodes behind ear measure brain's electrical activity
- AI-driven signal processing for mental state estimation
- iOS only (iPhone 12+)
- **No public API/SDK mentioned** — closed consumer ecosystem (device -> app)

## Scientific Foundation

- Built on decades of EEG and affective computing research
- Pilot work with Stanford Medicine, UCSF, longevity organizations
- NOT a medical device

## Pricing

## Hackathon Relevance

Real-time objective brain state data (stress, focus, mental load) could complement subjective mood reporting. Ground-truth signal for validating self-reported emotional states. Continuous passive monitoring vs. active check-ins.
